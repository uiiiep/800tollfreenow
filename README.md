## What Is 800 Toll Free Now?

800 Toll Free Now is a telecom service built specifically for small businesses. The company behind it — DIPVTEL — was founded by two people with over 30 years each in telecom. Their angle is simple: cut the big telecom middleman out of the equation and give small businesses access to wholesale-quality toll-free infrastructure at a fraction of the usual price.

## The "Unlimited" Thing — What's the Catch?

Fair question. The unlimited toll-free plans cap at **10,000 minutes per month** of forwarding. That sounds like a lot — and for most small businesses, it genuinely is. At 10k minutes, you'd need to average about 333 minutes of inbound calls per day to hit the ceiling. Most service businesses, law offices, repair shops, consultants — they're nowhere near that.

To put it in context: some of the bigger telecom players charge $50/month for just 250 minutes. Here you're getting 10,000 for $29.99. The math isn't subtle.

## The IVR Plans — Worth the Upgrade?

The Pro plans add an IVR (Interactive Voice Response) system — basically an auto-attendant that greets callers, lets them press 1 for sales, 2 for support, etc.

The real-world benefit beyond routing? It filters out telemarketers and robo-callers. A lot of them hang up when they hit an automated system. If you're getting spammed on your business line constantly, the IVR upgrade might actually pay for itself in peace of mind alone.

## Number Porting — Can You Bring Your Existing Number?

Yes. If you already have a toll-free number you love, you can port it over. The porting process runs 12 to 30 business days (that timeline is an FCC regulation — not something 800 Toll Free Now controls). The upside: they'll get you set up in their system beforehand, so you won't have a gap in service when the transfer completes.

## A Quick Note on the Vanity Number Process

If you're eyeing a specific vanity number, it's worth searching availability before ordering. Numbers can take up to 10 business days to be released by the current carrier, and that's also outside 800 Toll Free Now's control. They can hold a number for up to 30 days while you decide — after that, it goes back into circulation.

The **Choice 800** option ($75 one-time) is specifically for 800-prefix numbers, which are scarcer and more premium than 833/844/855/etc. If having a "1-800" number matters for your brand, that's the route.
